About me

Hey, I'm Frederik! I'm a Staff Engineer at Idura, where I work on privacy-preserving digital identity verification. Identity verification is useful for a lot of things such as fraud prevention and anti-money laundering. Privacy preservation means that people shouldn't have to reveal all of their personal information just to buy a beer.

A big part of my work consists of contributing to standards at the OpenID Foundation, the Internet Engineering Task Force and the World Wide Web Consortium. A smaller part consists of advising on standards, business models and public policy and legislation. I also do a lot of programming, mostly in F# and TypeScript.

I am interested in, and have experience with:

  • Formal verification of software
  • Software engineering tools for functional programming languages
  • Type theory for distributed systems (and in general)
  • Proof assistants (interactive theorem provers)
  • Static program analysis tools
  • Security properties of internet standards
  • Approaches for teaching formal methods
  • Structural proof theory

If you are a student interested in writing your thesis on one of these topics, feel free to contact me and we might be able to work something out!

Check out my GitHub page or my LinkedIn page to see what I might be up to.

Software

  • The Isabelle Cabal, which adds Isabelle code generation support to Cabal.
  • SeCaV Prover, an automated theorem prover for the SeCaV proof system.
    Joint work with Asta Halkjær From.
  • SeCaV Unshortener, a tool for translating proofs into formal SeCaV proofs.
    Joint work with Asta Halkjær From, Emmanuel André Ryom, Oliver Emil Bøving, and Jørgen Villadsen.
  • coq-microprover, an automated theorem prover for propositional logic.
  • A bidirectional typechecker for predicative System F, featuring complete ML-style type inference.
    Joint work with Sindri Pétur Ingimundarson.
  • c-ctl-check, a prototype model checker for CTL over constraint semirings.

Other stuff

Before that I worked with content management and pricing systems for travel products.

I was a member of the board of the small non-profit internet service provider K-Net from 2017 to 2021. I was vice chairman of the board for most of that time. I also used to volunteer as a Network Operations Engineer in the K-Net Operations Group.

I helped start the Vermilion Racing team at DTU, where I designed safety critical electronics and software for their electric vehicle.
